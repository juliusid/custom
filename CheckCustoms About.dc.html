Our Mission

Every importer in Nigeria should know exactly what clearing will cost — before their goods land.

That's the whole reason CheckCustoms.ng exists — and everything below is built around it.

3–5×
the spread we've seen between competing agent quotes for the same shipment.
The Problem We Saw
Clearing costs in Nigeria have never had a benchmark — so importers were left to guess, or trust a stranger's word.

Agent quotes vary wildly with no reference point. Finding a reliable agent is word-of-mouth and high-risk. And when an under-budgeted shipment sits in demurrage, the cash-flow shock lands on the importer, not the agent.

We built the benchmark that should have existed all along.

Why Now

The timing wasn't a coincidence.

Rising stakes
Import values are climbing, and so is the cost of getting a clearing estimate wrong.
Moving targets
Tariff and levy changes are frequent enough that a static rate card goes stale within a quarter.
Nobody owns it yet
Digital comfort among Nigerian importers is real — but no one has taken "check the cost, then clear it" end-to-end.
Who We Serve

Three kinds of people we build for.

The individual vehicle importer
He's bringing in one car from abroad and has exactly one chance to get the budget right before it lands at Tin-Can.
The SME goods importer
She runs recurring shipments and needs consistent, comparable numbers every time — not a fresh negotiation each container.
The licensed clearing agent
He's good at his job and wants steady, vetted work — without chasing leads or undercutting quotes to win it.
